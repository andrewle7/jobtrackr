Project: Topic Proposal

Problem Domain

For our problem domain, our team will be addressing the lack of data collection for the local Seattle community in regards to being able to seamlessly navigate thrift pop-ups stores, flea markets, and other means of sustainable and reusable clothing. Reusing clothing provides a zero waste, low cost option when shopping for apparel. The proliferation of recycled clothing establishments in the Greater Seattle area, arising from a large demand, has left the community with an array of options to purchase used/thrifted clothing. This has left an unmet need of an information technology solution, in terms of the community understanding what options are available to them (type) , as well as when and where these options will be locally available.

Although global, a previous solution that attempted to solve a similar issue is Thriftigo. Thriftigo found the best thrift stores near you, and allowed for users to share what was purchased to compare what has been thrifted at which locations. This mobile application did not succeed in identifying when community meet ups or other reusable clothing events took place , and therefore lacked a sense of community and inspiration within Seattle. Thriftigo has since been removed from the app store.

App Description

With the rising popularity of secondhand markets and goods, the industry for this in terms of reselling used goods has boomed. This has also led to a trend of emerging small businesses selling second hand “vintage” goods themselves. With nostalgia and social trends leading the forefront, the main users of this application will be those who are newer to the secondhand market, or those that are well-versed and need a hub to keep track of all flea markets happening in their area. This application could be used by the consumer as well as sellers of secondhand goods, making the demographic of the users extremely diverse in age and background. With exposure to these local flea markets being mainly word of mouth, or in complicated Seattle traveler guides, this application would allow a user to keep informed of everything going on, as well as support the local businesses that attend these events. With features such as filtering events happening in a specific area/radius of the user, they can find what is happening closest to them and when. Once the user finds a flea market they want to attend, they can create an account using their email, gmail, etc. and add it to a personal calendar, with the ones they want to go to displayed and saved in one place. Keeping in mind the demographic of sellers utilizing this app, there will be also be an option to add a flea market of your own that you are hosting to the pool, and increase exposure to the smaller events/markets that they may have going on as well.

Users will be viewing pop-ups/flea markets within the neighborhood and category they have filtered their search for. This will include the name, image, hours of business, and exact location of their desired search. This is going to help users find flea markets and pop-up shops easily and conveniently. Making it easy to find affordable stores will encourage people to reuse or resell their items instead of adding on to the waste problems we have today. For low-income individuals who use pop-ups or flea markets as their primary source of shopping, having knowledge of where each store is can give them additional sources of finding affordable apparel and other items they may need.
